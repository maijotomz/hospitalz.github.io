Gastric Balloon Surgery

  2. General Surgery

Description

The gastric balloon also known as intra gastric balloon (IGB) is a new FDA-approved minimally invasive procedure for weight loss. It involves inserting a balloon in your stomach to help give the feeling of fullness and reduce the appetite thereby resulting in weight loss.

Diagnosis

You are a good candidate for the gastric balloon surgery if:
  • Your body mass index (BMI) is between 30 and 40
  • You do not want a surgical procedure
  • You are ready to accept temporary implant in the stomach
  • You are willing to commit to the healthy lifestyle changes and regular medical follow-up as well as participate in the behavioural therapy
  • You have not had any previous stomach or esophageal surgery
  • You do not have heartburn
  • You do not have poorly stabilized diabetes type II
The gastric balloon surgery isn’t the right choice for every individual who is overweight. Discuss with your doctor to see if this procedure might be beneficial for you.

Treatment

Your healthcare team will provide you with specific instructions on how to prepare for your procedure to have an intragastric balloon placed in your stomach. You may be required to undergo various lab tests and exams before your procedure. You may need to restrict what you eat and drink and the medications you take in the time leading up to the procedure.This procedure is done in the endoscopy unit as an outpatient procedure and the patient will be sedated during this procedure.During a gastric balloon procedure, a thin tube called a catheter is loaded with an intragastric balloon down your throat into the stomach.The surgeon then advances an endoscope, which is a flexible tube with a camera attached down your throat into your stomach which allows them to see the balloon as he or she fills it with saline.

Aftercare

You will need two hours for recovery and observation. You may experience nausea for the first three days or until your body gets used to the balloon. You may feel minimal discomfort for the first few days and then a feeling of fullness. You can have small amounts of liquid starting about six hours post procedure. Generally, the liquid diet continues until the start of the second week. You’ll probably be able to start eating regular food around three weeks after the insertion of the intragastric balloon. You can return to the normal activity after being discharged. You will have the most success by following a healthy diet and exercise program.
