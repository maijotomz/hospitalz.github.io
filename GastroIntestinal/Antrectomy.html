Description

At present almost all antrectomies are performed as open procedures, which mean that they are done through a large incision in the patient's abdomen with the patient under general anesthesia. After the patient is anesthetized, a urinary catheter is placed to monitor urinary output, and a nasogastric tube is inserted.

Diagnosis/Preparation

Diagnosis of PUD and other stomach disorders begins with taking the patient's history, including a family history. In many cases the patient's primary care physician will order tests in order to narrow the diagnosis.

Medication

Treatment of peptic ulcers caused by H. pylori has changed its focus in recent years from lowering the level of acidity in the stomach to eradicating the bacterium.
